## QUESTION TYPES

### 1. Grammar Validation Questions

Participants ask if a given Esperanto sentence is grammatically correct.

**Format**: "[Sentence] is correct?"

**Examples**:
```
Q: "La granda knabo estas juna. is correct?"
A: Yes, correct. Means "The big boy is young."

Q: "Tom estas multe interesa is correct?"
A: Almost correct, but use "tre" instead of "multe" for "very interesting"

Q: "La knabinoj lernas rapidej. is correct?"
A: No, should be "rapide" (adverb ends in -e, not -ej)

Q: "La birdo estas ne granda is correct?"
A: Understandable but not standard. Should be "La birdo ne estas granda"
```

**Concepts Tested**:
- Adjective agreement
- Adverb formation (-e suffix)
- Negation word order
- Sentence structure

---

### 2. Translation/Meaning Questions

Participants ask for translations between Esperanto and other languages.

**Examples**:
```
Q: "Tom dormis bona meaning?"
A: Grammatically incorrect, but likely means "Tom slept well"
   (should be "Tom dormis bone")

Q: "Anna learnas Esperanto"
A: Not correct. Should be "Anna lernas Esperanton"
   (verb ends in -as, object in -on)

Q: "The bird flies"
A: "La birdo flugas"

Q: "The boy learns Esperanto"
A: "La knabo lernas Esperanton"

Q: "esperanto in cantonese?"
A: 世界语 (Sai3 Gai3 Yu5) or 愛斯不蘭多
```

**Concepts Tested**:
- Verb conjugation (-as present tense)
- Accusative case (-n suffix)
- Vocabulary
- Cross-language understanding

---

### 3. Grammar Concept Questions

Participants ask about specific Esperanto grammar rules.

**Examples**:
```
Q: "conjugation?"
A: Esperanto verbs don't change by subject. Tenses: -as (present),
   -is (past), -os (future), -us (conditional), -u (imperative)

Q: "How do verbs change in Esperanto?"
A: Only by tense, not by person/number

Q: "words end with -estas means what in esperanto?"
A: -estas is not a grammatical ending. "Estas" is the verb "to be"

Q: "what is to like in esperanto?"
A: "ŝati" - Mi ŝatas (I like), Vi ŝatas (you like), etc.

Q: "name in esperanto"
A: "nomo" (noun) or "nomi" (verb)
```

**Concepts Tested**:
- Verb conjugation system
- Regular verb patterns
- Common vocabulary
- Word formation rules

---

### 4. Conversation Sequencing Tasks

Participants receive a scrambled dialogue and must order it correctly.

**Example**:
```
Given scrambled conversation:
1. Marko: Saluton, instruisto! Mi fartas bone.
2. Teacher: Bone! Prenu la libron, kaj ni komencu la lecionon.
3. Teacher: Saluton, Marko! Kiel vi fartas hodiaŭ?
4. Teacher: Bone. Kion vi lernas nun?
5. Marko: Jes, instruisto!
6. Teacher: Ĉu vi lernas Esperanton rapide?
7. Marko: Jes, sur mia tablo estas libro kaj paperoj.
8. Teacher: Tre bone, Marko. Ĉu libro estas sur via tablo?
9. Marko: Mi lernas Esperanton kaj matematikon.
10. Marko: Jes, mi lernas rapide. La lecionoj estas interesaj!

Task: "Please write the numbers"

Correct order: 3, 1, 4, 9, 6, 10, 8, 7, 2, 5
(or similar logical flow)
```

**Concepts Tested**:
- Reading comprehension
- Conversational flow understanding
- Contextual reasoning
- Esperanto vocabulary in context

---

### 5. Error Identification and Correction

Participants identify what's wrong with incorrect sentences.

**Examples**:
```
Q: "Why is 'Anna learnas Esperanto' wrong?"
A: Should be "lernas" (not learnas) and "Esperanton" (accusative)

Q: "What's wrong with 'Tom dormis bona'?"
A: Should be "bone" (adverb, not adjective)

Q: "Fix: 'La knabinoj lernas rapidej'"
A: Change "rapidej" to "rapide" (adverb ending)
```

**Examples**:

Q: "esperanto uses?"

## GRAMMAR CONCEPTS COVERED

Based on analysis of 339 quiz conversations, the following Esperanto concepts are most frequently tested:

### Core Concepts (High Frequency)

1. **Verb Conjugation**
   - Present tense: -as
   - Past tense: -is
   - Future tense: -os
   - Regular conjugation (no person/number agreement)

2. **Adjective Formation**
   - All adjectives end in -a
   - Agreement with nouns
   - Predicative vs attributive use

3. **Adverb Formation**
   - All adverbs end in -e
   - Distinction from adjectives
   - Common mistake: using adjective instead of adverb

4. **Accusative Case**
   - Direct objects take -n suffix
   - Examples: "Esperanton" (Esperanto as object)
   - "Mi lernas Esperanton" vs "Esperanto estas facila"

5. **Negation**
   - Word "ne" before verb
   - "La birdo ne estas granda" (not "estas ne granda")

6. **Plurals**
   - Plural marker: -j
   - "knabo" (boy) → "knaboj" (boys)
   - "knabino" (girl) → "knabinoj" (girls)

### Secondary Concepts (Medium Frequency)

7. **Word Formation Suffixes**
   - -ej (place): kuirejo (kitchen), lernejo (school)
   - -in (feminine): knabino (girl), instruistino (female teacher)
   - -il (tool): manĝilo (eating utensil)

8. **Common Verbs**
   - esti (to be) → estas
   - lerni (to learn) → lernas
   - dormi (to sleep) → dormis
   - flugi (to fly) → flugas
   - ŝati (to like) → ŝatas

9. **Basic Vocabulary**
   - Numbers, colors, family terms
   - Common nouns: libro (book), tablo (table), birdo (bird)
   - Adjectives: granda (big), juna (young), interesa (interesting)

10. **Question Words**
    - Kio (what), Kiu (who), Kiel (how), Kie (where)
    - Ĉu (yes/no question marker)

## QUIZ ADMINISTRATION

### Student Journey

1. **ID Confirmation** (1-2 minutes)
   - Participant states ID in format: DDMMYYYY_HHMM_N
   - Example: "My ID is 05122024_1600_2"

2. **Optional Practice** (0-10 minutes)
   - Some participants ask preliminary questions
   - Weather, timestamps, general chat
   - Not all participants have practice phase

3. **Main Quiz** (10-30 minutes)
   - Esperanto grammar and translation questions
   - Interactive Q&A with AI
   - Immediate feedback on answers
   - Self-paced progression

4. **Completion**
   - Participant ends conversation when done
   - No explicit "submit" or "finish" marker
   - Identified by conversation end time

### Participant Behavior Patterns

**Single-session participants** (365/376 = 97%):
- Complete quiz in one continuous conversation
- Average duration: 13-14 minutes
- Typical pattern: ID → Questions → End

**Multi-session participants** (11/376 = 3%):
- Return across multiple days
- May ask follow-up questions
- Example: Participant 01122024_1500_11 had 8 sessions over 4 days
